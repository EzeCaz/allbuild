Services Overview  ·  For Business Owners

Eight ways we make the day easier — without ever feeling like software.

A plain-language guide to what your crew actually does. No jargon, no dashboards to learn, no new login to remember — just teammates who show up, pick up the load, and let you get back to the human work.

We do not sell "AI agents" or "orchestration layers." We sell time, calm, and the feeling of having someone in your corner. Every service below is one way we deliver on that promise — quietly, in the background, every working day. Pick the ones your business needs today; the rest are there when you grow into them.

1

The Coordinator

Originally: The Orchestrator

The teammate who makes sure nothing falls through the cracks. Every task gets picked up by the right person, every time. Two crew members never collide on the same job — and nothing waits silently for someone to notice it.

2

People Memory

Originally: Pule — People Context

We remember your customers, your suppliers, and your team — so you never have to explain a relationship twice. When someone messages, your crew already knows who they are, what they last ordered, and what they care about.

3

Knowledge at Hand

Originally: Albert — Knowledge

Your documents, past conversations, and meeting notes become a searchable library the whole team can draw on. No more digging through old emails. Ask a question, get an answer with a source.

4

Always-On Crew

Originally: Always-on Services

Background helpers that keep working when you're not. Scheduled tasks run on time, monitoring checks the things that matter, follow-ups happen automatically — so the morning starts with work already done.

5

Tool Connections

Originally: Tool Integrations

We plug into the tools you already use — email, calendar, tasks, messaging, content, and more. Nothing has to move. Your crew works inside the apps your business already lives in, alongside you.

6

The Dashboard

Originally: Operator Dashboard

One calm screen where you see what's done, what's in progress, and what needs your eyes. No noise, no fifty tabs. Everything important in one place, organized by what you actually need to do next.

7

Sign-Off Loop

Originally: Review & Automation Workflows

Anything that needs your judgment comes to you first. Nothing important ships without your say-so. Your crew checks its own work, flags anything uncertain, and waits for your nod before sending that email or posting that reply.

8

Crew Members

Originally: Agent Personas

Specialist teammates, each with a role — one for scheduling, one for follow-ups, one for research, one for bookkeeping. Bring the ones you need, hire more as you grow. They remember, they learn, they get better.

A crew that handles the busywork, so you can do the human work.
